Project 1: Analysis of Trader Joe's

Trader Joes

For this project, I will analyze trader Joe's website. I do so for the purpose of comparison; Does the website help complete the mission of Trader Joe's? I will be designing website elements for this class, and so analysis of websites will prove beneficial forr my own knowledge.

I will ask the following questions in my analysis:

  1. What was my first impression of this site?
  2. How does this site organize the flow of information? (Do they have links on the left and text on the right? Some other scheme? No scheme?)
  3. Does the site structure make sense?
  4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it?
  5. How long did it take for the home page to load?
  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?
  7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?
  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?
  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?
  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

1: First impression

The Trader Joe's website is a cheery site, offering media and recipes that go hand-in-hand with the local and festive theme of their store. The white color scheme and news bulletin add to the simple, friendly and fun interface. While a little bit confusing to navigate, my first impression was rather positive.

2: Site Organization

This is the only aspect of the site that gave me a negative impression. I found the abundance of small-town media to be a bit cluttering, and the way that the site only shows one new product at a time makes viewing what they have to offer tedious. The organization of the site makes navigating a bit of a learning curve.

3: Site Structure

Once you get the hang of the organization, it is easy to undestand the structure of the site. There are easy-to-find links for all necessary and common needs, and overall the structure of the site was well done.

4: Site Graphics/ Other Media

the site graphics, while unique, stylish, and exciting, also add to the cluttered feel of the site. The pictures can feel a bit random and add to the confusion.

5: Time to load

The site loaded quickly.

6: Text and Color

The text is clear, well organized with headings, and easy to read. It is also a font that adds to the bulletin board style.

7: Site Navigation

As I mentioned before, the site navigation can be tricky at first. There is a navigation tool, but the way that things are made to look like they were "posted" on the site makes information uneven, appearing in e=unexpected areas and intermingled with less useful information and graphics.

8: Color Scheme

The color scheme is white, and it is followed well throughout the site. The white scheme is another thing that I would change, as it makes the website feel less professional. The links and graphics make the site feel like a news board, why now allow for a cork-textured background? Any earthy-colored background would work well as well.

9: Responsive Design

The website does have a responsive design.

10: Uniqueness of Site

Overall, the website is a unique experience. Despite the confusion, it was well done and fun to navigate. The website is inviting, and makes me feel a certain kinship with the franchise, as if it were a small locally-owned store from my hometown.
